What Is Crisis Support?

Our crisis support services provide immediate assistance for individuals experiencing mental health emergencies, emotional distress, or suicidal thoughts. We offer a safe, confidential space to talk with trained professionals who can help you through difficult moments.

Crisis situations may include suicidal thoughts, self-harm urges, severe anxiety or panic attacks, traumatic experiences, or overwhelming emotional pain. You don't have to face these challenges alone - our team is here to listen, support, and guide you toward appropriate care.

All crisis support is provided by mental health professionals trained in crisis intervention. We prioritize your safety and wellbeing, offering both immediate support and connections to ongoing care when needed.

Recognizing Crisis Warning Signs

When to seek immediate help for yourself or others

Suicidal Thoughts

Expressing thoughts of suicide, death, or wanting to die, or making plans for suicide.

Self-Harm

Engaging in self-injury behaviors like cutting, burning, or other forms of self-harm.

Severe Depression

Overwhelming sadness, hopelessness, or withdrawal lasting more than two weeks.

Psychotic Symptoms

Experiencing hallucinations, delusions, or losing touch with reality.

Extreme Anxiety

Panic attacks, uncontrollable fear, or anxiety that interferes with daily functioning.

Substance Abuse

Dangerous drug or alcohol use that may lead to overdose or risky behaviors.

Trauma Reactions

Severe distress after experiencing or witnessing a traumatic event.

Violent Behavior

Threats or acts of violence toward oneself or others.

Frequently Asked Questions

Common questions about crisis support

When should I call a crisis line versus going to the emergency room?

Call a crisis line if you're experiencing emotional distress, suicidal thoughts, or need someone to talk to. Go to the emergency room or call emergency services if there's immediate danger to life (active suicide attempt, medical emergency, or violence occurring). Crisis counselors can help you determine the appropriate level of care if you're unsure.

Will calling a crisis line get me hospitalized?

Hospitalization is only considered when there's imminent risk to life that can't be managed safely otherwise. Our goal is to help you stay safe in the least restrictive environment possible. Crisis counselors will work with you to create a safety plan and explore all options before considering hospitalization.

What if I don't feel comfortable talking on the phone?

We offer text-based crisis support through our online chat service. This allows you to communicate with a crisis counselor via text if that feels more comfortable. Many people find texting easier when they're in distress or have difficulty speaking about their feelings.

Are crisis services available in different languages?

Yes! Our crisis services are available in Amharic, Afaan Oromoo, Tigrinya, Arabic, and English. When you call or text, you can request support in your preferred language, and we'll connect you with a counselor who speaks that language or use translation services if needed.
