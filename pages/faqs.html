How do I know if I should seek professional mental health help?

You might consider professional help if you experience:

  • Persistent feelings of sadness or hopelessness
  • Difficulty performing daily tasks
  • Significant changes in sleep or appetite
  • Withdrawal from social activities
  • Thoughts of self-harm or suicide
  • Substance abuse issues

Our self-assessment tool can help you evaluate whether professional support might be beneficial.

Are your services confidential?

Yes, all our services are completely confidential. We adhere to strict privacy policies and ethical guidelines to protect your information. The only exceptions are:

  • If we believe you are at risk of harming yourself or others
  • Cases involving child or vulnerable adult protection concerns
  • When required by court order

We will always discuss confidentiality with you at your first session.

What's the difference between counseling and psychiatric treatment?

Counseling (or therapy) and psychiatric treatment are complementary but different:

Counseling/Therapy Psychiatric Treatment
Focus Talk therapy, coping strategies, behavior change Medication management, biological aspects
Provider Psychologist, counselor, social worker Psychiatrist (medical doctor)
Approach Psychological techniques Medical model

Many people benefit from a combination of both approaches.

How can I support a loved one with mental health challenges?

Supporting a loved one with mental health challenges requires patience and understanding. Here are some ways to help:

  • Educate yourself about their condition
  • Listen without judgment
  • Encourage professional help without forcing
  • Offer practical support with daily tasks
  • Take care of your own mental health
  • Be patient with their recovery process

Are there cultural considerations in your mental health services?

Absolutely. We recognize that mental health is deeply influenced by cultural context. Our approach includes:

  • Multilingual counselors familiar with Ethiopian cultures
  • Respect for religious and spiritual beliefs
  • Understanding of family dynamics in Ethiopian society
  • Cultural sensitivity in all our programs
  • Community-based approaches to mental health

We continuously work to make our services culturally appropriate and accessible.

What payment options do you accept for counseling services?

We offer several payment options to make our services accessible:

  • Cash payments
  • Mobile money (M-Pesa, Telebirr)
  • Bank transfer
  • Sliding scale fees based on income
  • Some insurance plans (please inquire)

We never turn anyone away due to inability to pay - please contact us to discuss options.

How do I access your online resources and community forum?

Accessing our online resources is simple:

  1. Visit our website and create a free account
  2. Browse the Resources section for self-help tools
  3. Join the Community Forum to connect with others
  4. Download any available PDF guides or worksheets
  5. Participate in our online workshops and events
